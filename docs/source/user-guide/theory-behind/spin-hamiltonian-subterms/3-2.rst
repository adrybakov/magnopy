***************
(2, 1, 0) terms
***************

Second case of the trilinear terms, where two spin operators are associated with the same
site, and the third one is associated with a different site. In this page we imply that
:math:`\boldsymbol{r}_{\mu,\alpha_1} \neq \boldsymbol{r}_{\mu + \nu_2, \alpha_2}`.

.. math::
    C_{3,2}
    \sum_{\substack{\mu, \nu_2, \\ \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \\ i_1, i_2, i_3}}
    \Biggl(
        &
        J^{i_1, i_2, i_3}_{0, \nu_2; \alpha_1, \alpha_1, \alpha_2}
        S_{\mu, \alpha_1}^{i_1}
        S_{\mu, \alpha_1}^{i_2}
        S_{\mu + \nu_2, \alpha_2}^{i_3}
        +\\&+
        J^{i_1, i_2, i_3}_{\nu_2, 0; \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_1}
        S_{\mu, \alpha_1}^{i_1}
        S_{\mu + \nu_2, \alpha_2}^{i_2}
        S_{\mu, \alpha_1}^{i_3}
        +\\&+
        J^{i_1, i_2, i_3}_{\nu_2, \nu_2; \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_2}
        S_{\mu, \alpha_1}^{i_1}
        S_{\mu + \nu_2, \alpha_2}^{i_2}
        S_{\mu + \nu_2, \alpha_2}^{i_3}
    \Biggr)
